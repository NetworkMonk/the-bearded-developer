# Backlinks Strategy — The Bearded Developer

A working checklist for building domain authority and generating more inbound leads.
Tick off items as they are completed. Revisit and update quarterly.

---

## Priority Order

1. Platform ecosystem listings (highest value, you already qualify)
2. B2B agency directories (low effort, consistent backlinks)
3. Content-led link building (ongoing, compounds over time)
4. Community participation (nofollow but drives real referral traffic)
5. PR and thought leadership (hardest, highest authority)

---

## Tier 1 — Platform Ecosystem Listings

These are the most valuable links available to you. You already qualify for most of them.

### Celigo Partner Directory
- [ ] Confirm partner status and tier with Celigo account manager
- [ ] Ensure profile is live on the Celigo partner/find-a-partner page
- [ ] Verify the link points to the correct service page (`/services/celigo`)
- [ ] Add Celigo partner badge to site where appropriate

### BigCommerce Partner Directory
- [ ] Confirm agency partner status with BigCommerce
- [ ] Ensure profile is live on the BigCommerce partner finder
- [ ] Verify link points to `/services/bigcommerce`
- [ ] Add BigCommerce partner badge to site

### Shopify Experts Marketplace
- [ ] Confirm Expert listing is live and up to date
- [ ] Ensure specialisms listed match current services (integrations, NetSuite, development)
- [ ] Verify link points to correct landing page
- [ ] Request reviews from Shopify clients through the platform

### NetSuite / Oracle Partner Listings
- [ ] Check if Solution Provider or SuiteApp listing is available
- [ ] Apply if not already listed — link from oracle.com is high value
- [ ] Ensure listing covers integration and e-commerce specialisms

## Tier 2 — B2B Agency Directories

Minimal effort, consistent backlinks, cumulative review equity over time.

### Clutch.co ⭐ (Most Important)
- [ ] Create or claim company profile at clutch.co
- [ ] Fill in all sections: services, team size, hourly rate, client focus, portfolio
- [ ] Add case study examples where possible
- [ ] **Identify 5 past clients to request verified reviews from** — Clutch contacts them directly for a short interview
- [ ] Send client outreach emails requesting reviews
- [ ] Once 3+ reviews are live, check ranking position in relevant Clutch lists (e.g. "NetSuite agencies UK", "Shopify developers UK")
- [ ] Embed Clutch review widget on site once available

### G2
- [ ] Create profile in the G2 Services section at g2.com
- [ ] Select relevant categories: ERP Services, Shopify Development, System Integration Services, E-commerce Development
- [ ] Fill in full profile including description and service areas
- [ ] **Send clients a review request link** — they complete a simple form, no phone call required
- [ ] Aim for 5+ reviews to qualify for ranked lists
- [ ] Embed G2 badge on site once earned

### GoodFirms
- [ ] Create profile at goodfirms.co
- [ ] Fill in services, team size, hourly rate range, and portfolio examples
- [ ] **Request reviews from 3–5 clients** (simple form, no phone interview)
- [ ] Once reviews are live, **apply to be included in relevant Top Lists** (e.g. "Top NetSuite Development Companies UK", "Top Celigo Implementation Partners")
- [ ] Monitor for inclusion in any editorial round-up articles they publish

### Additional Directories (Lower Priority)
- [ ] DesignRush — list under e-commerce, NetSuite, and Shopify specialisms
- [ ] Sortlist — UK-focused, good for agency discovery
- [ ] Expertise.io
- [ ] The Manifest (sister site to Clutch)

## Tier 3 — Content-Led Link Building

The articles already published are link-building assets. This section is about actively distributing them.

### Guest Post Targets

For each target, identify a relevant existing article, pitch a complementary piece, and offer the published version as a starting draft.

- [ ] **Practical Commerce** (practicalecommerce.com) — e-commerce how-to content from practitioners. Pitch integration or platform topics.
- [ ] **Econsultancy** — UK digital marketing and e-commerce publication. Pitch a piece on NetSuite or Celigo for mid-market retailers.
- [ ] **NetSuite Community Blog / Oracle Blog** — partner-written content sometimes featured. Contact partner manager.
- [ ] **BigCommerce Blog** — accepts partner contributions on technical topics. Contact BigCommerce partner team.
- [ ] **Shopify Plus Blog** — similar to above. Pitch through Shopify Expert account.
- [ ] **eDelivery / InternetRetailing** — UK retail technology publications. Good for integration and automation topics.

### Skyscraper Outreach

Find pages that rank for target keywords but are thin or outdated. Reach out to sites linking to them and suggest your article as a better resource.

- [ ] Search "BigCommerce NetSuite integration" — identify weak articles ranking page 1
- [ ] Search "Celigo vs MuleSoft" — identify comparison articles that are outdated or superficial
- [ ] Search "digital support retainer" — identify any existing content and sites that link to it
- [ ] For each: find sites linking to weak articles (use Ahrefs or free alternative like Ubersuggest)
- [ ] Draft short outreach email suggesting your article as a replacement/supplement

### Outreach Email Template

```
Subject: Better resource for your [topic] article

Hi [Name],

I noticed you link to [URL] from your article on [topic].

We've just published a more comprehensive guide on the same subject that your readers might find more useful: [your article URL]

It covers [brief description of what makes it better].

Happy to share the link — let me know if it's a fit.

Best,
James
```

## Tier 4 — Community Participation

These are often nofollow links but drive real referral traffic and build brand recognition with buyers who are actively looking for help.

- [ ] **NetSuite Community** (community.oracle.com) — answer integration and e-commerce questions; link to relevant articles where genuinely helpful
- [ ] **BigCommerce Community Forum** — same approach; particularly useful for technical questions about integrations
- [ ] **Shopify Community** — active forum; link to articles when they answer someone's specific question
- [ ] **Reddit — r/netsuite** — answer questions, mention content where relevant; do not spam
- [ ] **Reddit — r/ecommerce** — similar approach; integration and platform questions come up regularly
- [ ] **Reddit — r/shopify** — high volume of questions; link to relevant articles when they directly answer a question
- [ ] **LinkedIn Articles** — republish articles on LinkedIn with "read the full version" CTA linking back to site; reaches decision-makers directly
- [ ] Set up a regular cadence: one LinkedIn article per fortnight from existing content

## Tier 5 — PR and Thought Leadership

Harder to achieve but generates the highest authority links from trusted publications.

### HARO / Expert Platforms
- [ ] Sign up to **HARO** (Help a Reporter Out) at helpareporter.com — free tier available
- [ ] Sign up to **Qwoted** at qwoted.com — similar platform, often higher quality requests
- [ ] Sign up to **Featured.com** — structured expert Q&A, good for agency positioning
- [ ] Set up daily email digest and respond quickly to relevant requests (e-commerce, ERP, integration, SME technology topics)
- [ ] Target: 1–2 responses per week; even 1 placement per month in a DA 50+ publication moves the needle

### Podcast Appearances
- [ ] **2X Ecommerce Podcast** — e-commerce operations and technology focus; pitch as Celigo/NetSuite integration specialist
- [ ] **Add to Cart Podcast** — e-commerce focused; pitch integration/automation episode
- [ ] **Ecommerce Fastlane** — Shopify-focused; pitch a technical or integration topic
- [ ] Research 3–5 UK e-commerce podcasts and prepare a pitch: one sentence on who you are, one sentence on the topic you can speak to, one sentence on why their audience would find it useful

### Trade Press
- [ ] **Retail Gazette** — UK retail technology; pitch comment pieces on integration topics
- [ ] **InternetRetailing** — UK e-commerce publication; pitch expert opinion pieces
- [ ] **eDelivery** — UK fulfilment and e-commerce logistics; relevant for ERP/integration content

Use this to ask clients to leave reviews across all platforms in one go:

```
Subject: Quick favour — would you leave us a review?

Hi [Name],

It's been great working with you on [project]. We're building up our presence on a
few agency review platforms and would really appreciate a short review if you have
5–10 minutes.

Here are the links:

- Clutch: [link] — they may follow up with a short call
- G2: [link] — quick form, no call required
- GoodFirms: [link] — simple form, takes a couple of minutes

Even one or two reviews makes a real difference. Thank you in advance.

Best,
James
```

## Tracking and Review

- [ ] Set up a simple spreadsheet to track all directory listings (URL, date submitted, review count, link status)
- [ ] Check Clutch, G2, and GoodFirms rankings quarterly for target categories
- [ ] Use Google Search Console to monitor which articles are attracting inbound links
- [ ] Review this checklist quarterly and update with new opportunities

---

## What to Avoid

- Do not buy links — Google's spam detection is sophisticated and penalties are very hard to recover from
- Do not submit to irrelevant or low-quality directories — quantity without relevance adds noise, not signal
- Do not use generic link exchange schemes
- Do not republish full articles on LinkedIn (or anywhere) without a canonical link back — duplicate content can cannibalise your own rankings
